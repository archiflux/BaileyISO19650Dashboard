1.0

Introduction

Bailey Partnership Group Limited is committed to implementing best practice information management in line with BS EN ISO 19650 standards. This policy establishes our approach to managing information throughout the lifecycle of built assets.

üéØ
Policy Purpose

This Information Management Policy defines how Bailey Partnership manages, controls, and delivers information across all projects, ensuring compliance with ISO 19650 principles and UK BIM Framework requirements.

1.1

Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All BIM and information management projects
  • Design, construction, operation, and maintenance phases
  • All disciplines within Bailey Partnership
  • Roles as both Lead Appointed Party and Appointed Party
  • All project team members, consultants, and subcontractors
1.2

UK BIM Framework Alignment

This policy aligns with the UK Information Management Framework, covering:

  • Information Creation: Standards and procedures for generating project information
  • Information Management: CDE workflows, version control, and access management
  • Information Storage: Structured data repositories and backup procedures
  • Information Disposal: Archiving and retention policies in compliance with legal requirements
2.0

Objectives

Information Management
  • Implement ISO 19650 principles across all projects
  • Standardize information requirements and delivery
  • Ensure data quality, integrity, and security
  • Optimize information exchange between stakeholders
Project Delivery
  • Improve project outcomes through better information management
  • Reduce errors and rework through quality assurance
  • Enhance collaboration and coordination
  • Support decision-making with timely, accurate information
Technical Standards
  • Adopt industry-standard data formats (IFC, COBie)
  • Implement UK BIM Framework naming conventions
  • Use Uniclass 2015 classification where applicable
  • Maintain interoperability between systems
Continuous Improvement
  • Capture and apply lessons learned
  • Regular review and update of procedures
  • Investment in staff training and development
  • Monitor industry developments and standards
3.0

Key Principles

3.1

Information Management Functions

Bailey Partnership follows ISO 19650 information management functions:

Function Description Key Activities
Information Creation Generating project information to required standards Authoring, modeling, drawing production, specification
Information Container Management Organizing and structuring information Naming, classification, version control, metadata
Quality Management Ensuring information meets requirements QA checks, reviews, validation, verification
Security Management Protecting information from unauthorized access Access control, encryption, backup, audit trails
Collaborative Working Facilitating team coordination CDE workflows, coordination meetings, clash detection
3.2

Core Principles

Collaboration

Open communication, shared understanding, coordinated working practices, and trust-based relationships with all project stakeholders.

Standardization

Consistent processes, naming conventions, classification systems, and quality standards across all projects and disciplines.

Interoperability

Open data formats (IFC, COBie), software compatibility, seamless data exchange between systems and stakeholders.

Quality Control

Rigorous checking procedures, validation processes, regular reviews, and continuous improvement mechanisms.

Security

Data protection, controlled access, secure transmission, backup procedures, and compliance with GDPR and data protection laws.

Continuous Improvement

Lessons learned capture, process refinement, technology adoption, and staff development programs.

6.0

Information Quality Assurance

Bailey Partnership operates a Quality Management System (QMS) aligned with ISO 9001, ensuring information quality throughout the project lifecycle.

6.1

Information Quality Standards

Accuracy
Information must be correct, error-free, and reflect current project status
Completeness
All required information provided to specified level of detail
Consistency
Uniform standards, naming, and classification across all information
Timeliness
Information delivered according to TIDP milestones
Relevance
Appropriate to project stage and stakeholder requirements
Understandability
Clear, well-organized, and accessible to intended audience
6.2

QA Processes

Stage QA Activities Responsibility
Creation/Capture Author self-checks, adherence to standards, naming conventions Task Team Members
Validation/Verification Technical reviews, clash detection, compliance checks Task Team Manager / BIM Manager
Storage/Retrieval CDE workflows, version control, metadata verification Information Manager
Dissemination Approval workflows, authorization checks, access control Project Lead / Directors
Review/Update Periodic reviews, status updates, archiving procedures All Team Members
6.3

Continuous Improvement

Quality is maintained through:

  • Regular audits and compliance monitoring
  • Lessons learned from completed projects
  • Staff training and competency development
  • Client feedback and satisfaction surveys
  • Process efficiency metrics and KPI tracking
  • Annual policy review and updates
